# Ethics Gate Checklist (Goal 1) — Reusable for Future IT Projects

This checklist is a practical “pre-launch ethics gate” I will use to ensure future projects align with industry ethical practices and Indigenous-led principles where relevant. It is designed to be completed at **project start**, **before deployment**, and **before public release**.

---

## 1) Define the project and scope
- [ ] What is the project objective and why are we doing it?
- [ ] What data, systems, or communities does it affect?
- [ ] What decisions will be automated, and what decisions remain human?
- [ ] What is out of scope (explicitly)?

## 2) Stakeholder identification (including Indigenous stakeholders)
- [ ] Who is directly affected? Who is indirectly affected?
- [ ] Are Indigenous Australians stakeholders (users, communities, data subjects, cultural owners)?
- [ ] Who has cultural authority or governance responsibility?
- [ ] Have I identified power imbalances (who benefits vs who bears risk)?

## 3) Indigenous engagement and FPIC (if Indigenous stakeholders/data/cultural expression is involved)
**FPIC = Free, Prior and Informed Consent**
- [ ] **Free:** engagement is voluntary, not pressured or “rushed”
- [ ] **Prior:** consent obtained before building/releasing
- [ ] **Informed:** stakeholders understand purpose, risks, downstream use, and controls
- [ ] **Consent:** approval is documented and conditions are respected
- [ ] Engagement is culturally safe (appropriate communication, timeframes, and representation)
- [ ] Cultural authority is respected (what is not acceptable is clearly defined)
- [ ] Any ongoing governance/oversight is agreed (not one-off consultation)

## 4) Indigenous Data Sovereignty and data governance (if Indigenous data is involved)
- [ ] Data sources are documented (where it came from, how it was obtained)
- [ ] Ownership and rights are clear (who controls use and access)
- [ ] Data minimisation is applied (only collect what is necessary)
- [ ] Storage, access, retention, and deletion rules are defined
- [ ] Downstream sharing/secondary use is controlled or prohibited where needed
- [ ] A complaints/takedown pathway exists if harm occurs or concerns are raised

## 5) Privacy and data protection
- [ ] Privacy risks are identified (personal data exposure, re-identification, logging sensitivity)
- [ ] Controls are in place (least privilege, access logging, encryption, secure storage)
- [ ] Only relevant data is accessed/retained (purpose limitation)
- [ ] Stakeholders are informed where appropriate (transparency)

## 6) Intellectual property, copyright, and licensing
- [ ] I have confirmed IP ownership and licensing for all content/data used
- [ ] Training data (if using AI) is consented/licensed where required
- [ ] Attribution requirements are met
- [ ] Licensing terms prevent harmful or misleading reuse where feasible
- [ ] “Publicly available” is not treated as “permission” by default

## 7) Ethical hacking and security testing boundaries (if applicable)
- [ ] Testing is authorised and scoped (written permission / ticket / approval)
- [ ] Non-destructive methods are prioritised before exploitation
- [ ] A rollback plan exists for changes
- [ ] Findings are documented responsibly and shared through proper channels

## 8) Risk assessment and escalation
- [ ] Key harms/risks are identified (community harm, bias, misuse, reputational risk, legal risk)
- [ ] Mitigations are defined and realistic
- [ ] Residual risk is accepted by the appropriate approver (not informally)
- [ ] Escalation path is clear (who decides, who signs off, who is informed)

## 9) Documentation and accountability
- [ ] Ethical reasoning is documented (why decisions were made)
- [ ] Approvals/sign-offs are recorded
- [ ] Review date is set (post-implementation check)
- [ ] Monitoring plan exists (how we detect harm/misuse after release)
